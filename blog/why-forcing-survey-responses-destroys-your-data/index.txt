a:T8ae,<p class="mb-6">I received a survey from the BBC today. Probably like millions of other people.</p><p class="mt-6 mb-6">Like everyone else, I don't respond to every survey in my inbox, but I actually wanted to participate in this one.</p>
<p class="mb-6">The survey was long, so (again, like everyone else) I instinctively allocated a reasonable amount of time to complete it by answering the questions where I had a clear opinion, and skipping those where I didn't.</p><p class="mt-6 mb-6">To my surprise, I wasn't allowed to submit it like that.</p><div class="h-6"></div>
<ul>
<li>
<p class="mb-6">❌ <em>All the questions were mandatory.</em></p>
</li>
<li>
<p class="mb-6">❌ <em>No skipping allowed.</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="mb-6">In other words, I was <strong>forced</strong> to have an opinion about everything the BBC asked.</p><p class="mt-6 mb-6">This is a very common mistake companies make when designing surveys: they assume that customers are as invested in the questionnaire as the team that designed it. Spoiler alert: they're not.</p><div class="h-8"></div><figure class="my-8"><img src="/images/BBC-survey.png" alt="BBC survey page with 22 errors"><figcaption class="text-sm text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-400 italic text-left mt-2 mb-6">Respondents can't submit their honest answers.</figcaption></figure>
<p class="mb-6">If you <em>force</em> people to answer every question, even when they don't have a real opinion, you are <em>forcing</em> them to give random answers.</p>
<ul>
<li>❌ Random responses contaminate your data.</li>
<li>❌ Contaminated data will ruin your analysis.</li>
<li>❌ The whole project ends up (or should end up) in the bin, along with the budget you spent on it.</li>
</ul>
<p class="mb-6">Instead:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p class="mb-6">✅ <em>Make sure you don't force respondents. You can't force real feedback.</em></p>
</li>
<li>
<p class="mb-6">✅ <em>Prioritise the quality of responses, not the quantity.</em></p>
</li>
<li>
<p class="mb-6">✅ <em>Only make a question mandatory if it's critical to the logic of the survey.</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="mb-6">It's a hard lesson that many companies, including, sadly, today, the BBC, still haven't learned.</p>
